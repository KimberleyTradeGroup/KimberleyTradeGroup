Legal Framework

Built to
Protect
Everyone.

Every KTG engagement is governed by a clear, structured legal framework. Our role, liability, and obligations are explicitly defined before any introduction is made. There is no ambiguity — by design.

01
Commercial Agency Agreement
Clearly defines our role as introducer only. We take no title, make no representations, and bear zero liability for goods quality or transaction outcome. Our liability is capped to commission received on that transaction.
02
Non-Circumvention Agreement
All introduced relationships are contractually protected for 2 years. Neither party can bypass KTG's facilitation once an introduction is made — with a clear, proportionate remedy clause for any breach.
03
Letter of Credit Settlement
All payments are structured via regulated bank-backed Letters of Credit — not informal platforms. Funds are secured at a regulated institution before any goods or documentation move.
Common Questions

Everything
You Need
to Know.

We operate with full transparency. If your question is not answered here, reach out directly and we will respond within 24 hours.

What does Kimberley Trade Group actually do?+
We are an independent trade facilitator. We identify Australian suppliers, introduce them to qualified Indian buyers via the Malaysian corridor, and manage the documentation and communication flow throughout. We take no title to goods and earn a 5% commission only upon successful transaction completion. We are the architect, not the contractor.
Do I need to pay anything upfront?+
No. Our fee is strictly success-based — 5% of gross transaction value, payable only when a transaction completes. There is no retainer, no consulting fee, and no upfront cost for either the buyer or the seller. We earn only when you do.
How does AI-ECTA benefit me as an Indian buyer?+
The AI-ECTA agreement eliminates or significantly reduces import duties on a wide range of Australian goods entering India. Sourcing through KTG's corridor is already cheaper than sourcing via Dubai or Singapore — before any price negotiation. The advantage is structural and only widens as ECTA tariff reductions are progressively phased in.
How is payment handled? Is it safe?+
All transactions are settled via Letter of Credit (LC) through regulated banking institutions in both Australia and India. This is the global standard for secure international trade — funds are verified and secured at the bank level before goods move. We do not use informal escrow platforms or peer-to-peer payment systems.
What is KTG doing in Malaysia?+
KTG is actively building its Malaysian corridor infrastructure — conducting due diligence on Port Klang logistics operators, establishing relationships with freight forwarders and mill operators, and qualifying corridor partners ahead of Q3 2026 commodity allocation flows. Malaysia functions as the strategic transshipment pivot point of our AUS–IND corridor. All Malaysian introductions are NCNDA-protected.
Can I approach a supplier you've introduced directly in future?+
All parties sign our Non-Circumvention Agreement prior to introduction. This protects both the relationship and our facilitation role for 2 years. After that period, both parties are free to transact independently. The agreement is clear, proportionate, and governed by WA commercial law.
